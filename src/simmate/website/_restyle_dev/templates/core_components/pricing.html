TL;DR

Simmate is entirely free & open-source . Costs occur only when you:

  • Use our cloud resources to run workflows (~50¢ per CPU-hr)
  • Have our team set up & maintain a private server

USDC is for compute costs; Tokens are free & for budget tracking.

Data Access

All data is accessible for free.

Many datasets are made available by third-parties — such as the Materials Project, OQMD, JARVIS, and more. We will never charge for the access of data that is already free & open. Be sure to thank and properly attribute the original communities!

Importantly, some datasets are only available in the Self-Hosted version of Simmate — such as AFLOW, eMolecules, Enamine, and more. This is because those third-parties do not allow redistribution of their data. While we cannot display this data on our public website, the Simmate python package still lets you download datasets directly from third-party providers for your private use.

Compute Costs

Compute costs are based on the total resources and time used by a workflow run. Estimates are shown before submission, and charges apply only after the calculation completes successfully.

Costs can vary significantly because:

  • Not all CPU time is equal (e.g., 24 hrs on a 2GB RAM node is much cheaper than 24 hrs on a 32GB RAM large-mem node)
  • Total CPU usage can vary even for identical workflows (e.g., due to differences in DFT convergence or relaxation)
  • CPU time heavily depends on the submitted structure/molecule atom count and/or composition

This is why each workflow provides cost estimates and historical runtime data on its submission page.

Currently, all computations run on Kubernetes Pods hosted on DigitalOcean. Our pricing aligns closely with DigitalOcean’s rates, averaging about $0.50 per CPU hour.

Compute costs are paid using your USDC Balance. Do not confuse this with your Token Balance. (see section below)
Looking ahead, Simmate plans to integrate HPC clusters from partner companies and universities. Once available, these will reduce compute costs drastically. There are also plans to allow users to provide their own resources for their own calculation or even others to utilize. For the latter, Simmate would compensate contributed resources by adding to their USDC balance. Reach out if you are interested in early testing of these features.

Digital Currencies

USDC

1 USDC = $1 for compute costs & workflows

Simmate is in a very early stage where we would like to keep forms of payment limited. For now, the easiest route is to accept USDC, which is a stablecoin pegged 1:1 to the U.S. dollar. Using USDC allows peer-to-peer payments on the Ethereum blockchain without requiring any of your personal payment information — meaning no credit or debit card info needed.

If you’re new to crypto and USDC, we recommend using the Base app to create your wallet, fund, and send USDC.

Tokens

1 Token = $1 of estimated costs outside of Simmate

As an example, let's say you submit a molecule to the "Synthesis Tracker" app, indicating that you plan to run a synthesis of it. This costs nothing to submit & track in the website, but you may still want to record the total costs of actually running this synthesis in the lab (e.g., labor, reagents, etc.). You track these costs using Tokens. And as you record many syntheses and assay runs, you will be able to track real-world costs dedicated to specific projects, regions chemical chemical space, or specific hypotheses.

Thus, Simmate tokens have no physical value and are only used for planning and budgeting. Your token balance is unlimited since Tokens are purely theoretical, and you can adjust your balance in the navbar.
