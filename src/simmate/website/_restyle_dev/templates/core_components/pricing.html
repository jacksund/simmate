Data Access

All data is accessible for free.

Many datasets are made available by third-parties — such as the Materials Project, OQMD, JARVIS, and more. We will never charge for the access of data that is already free & open. Be sure to thank & properly attribute the original communities!

Importantly, some datasets are only available in the Self-Hosted version of Simmate — such as AFLOW, eMolecules, Enamine, and more. This is because those third-parties do not allow redistribution of their data. While we cannot display this data on our public website, the Simmate Python client still lets you download datasets directly from third-party providers for your private use.

Compute Costs

Compute costs are based on the total resources and time used by a workflow run. Estimates are shown before submission, and charges apply only after the calculation completes successfully.

Costs can vary significantly because:

  • Not all CPU time is equal (e.g., 24 hrs on a 2GB RAM node is much cheaper than 24 hrs on a 32GB RAM large-mem node)
  • Total CPU usage can vary even for identical workflows (e.g., due to differences in DFT convergence or relaxation)
  • CPU time heavily depends on the submitted structure/molecule atom count and/or composition

This is why each workflow provides cost estimates and historical runtime data on its submission page.

Currently, all computations run on Kubernetes Pods hosted on DigitalOcean. Our pricing aligns closely with DigitalOcean’s rates, averaging about $0.50 per CPU hour.

Compute costs are paid using your USDC Balance. Do not confuse this with your Token Balance. (see "USDC vs. Tokens" tab at the top of this page)
Looking ahead, Simmate plans to integrate HPC clusters from partner companies and universities. Once available, these will reduce compute costs drastically. There are also plans to allow users to provide their own resources for their own calculation or even others to utilize. For the latter, Simmate would compensate contributed resources by adding to their USDC balance. Reach out if you are interested in early testing of these features.
