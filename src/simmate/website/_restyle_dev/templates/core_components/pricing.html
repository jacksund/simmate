TL;DR

Simmate is entirely open-source , and all data is shared freely. So costs occur only when...

  • You use our cloud resources to run workflows, rather than your own
  • You have our team set up & maintain a private server

USDC is for compute costs; Tokens are free & for budget tracking.

Our Not-For-Profit Mission

Simmate is a "not-for-profit" organization, where our primary goal is to build a community that openly shares chemistry data. We believe that energy is wasted and research is slowed when academics repeatedly perform the same calculations in isolation, so we sought to build a platform that provides the infrastructure for open collaboration in chemistry. This is why in our public website you only pay for computational resources that are not your own, and any workflows ran on the public website are openly accessible to everyone. However, for the majority of researchers who work with confidential data, we still wish to collaborate through building the chemistry infrastructure together. For this reason, 100% of our codebase is open-source, and it is free to setup you own private server. So in summary, we aim to accelerate chemistry research through openness and collective innovation. Please reach out if you have any questions or are looking for new ways to contribute!

Data Access

All data is accessible for free.

And if you are on our public website, free data access includes *all* calculations ever submitted by *all* users. We recognize that this is undesirable for many research groups, but be aware you can still download Simmate and manage your database+compute privately. The public site will remain focused on our mission of openness.

Also, note that many of our datasets are made available through third-parties — such as the Materials Project, OQMD, JARVIS, and more. We will never charge for the access of data that is already free & open. Be sure to thank and properly attribute the original communities!

Importantly, some datasets are only available in the Self-Hosted version of Simmate — such as AFLOW, eMolecules, Enamine, and more. This is because those third-parties do not allow redistribution of their data. While we cannot display this data on our public website, the Simmate python package still lets you download datasets directly from third-party providers for your private use.

Compute Costs

Compute costs are based on the total resources and time used by a workflow run. The price is listed after providing your input structure(s)/molecules(s), and payment is carried out as soon as the workflow is submitted. Note, a refund is made immediately in cases where the calculation fails or after a refund-request review when results are unsatisfactory.

It is important to recognize that price is not determined until you provide all input parameters (including your structure/molecule). This is because total CPU usage can vary even for identical workflows (e.g., due to differences in DFT convergence or relaxation). However, the expected CPU time heavily depends on the submitted structure/molecule atom count or composition, meaning we can provide fair pricing before calculation submission.

This is counter to other cloud platforms that charge at the end after the calculation completes or at a fixed rate throughout the computation. We opt for the pay-first method for several reasons:

  • Users can submit with confidence and avoid surprises.
  • Clear pricing allows for easier project budgeting and management.
  • We never want to wastefully terminating workflows mid-run due to insufficient funds.
  • It reduces our administrative overhead, keeping the platform simple and fair.

Overall, this is why each workflow provides cost estimates and historical runtime data on its submission page.

Currently, all computations run on Kubernetes Pods hosted on DigitalOcean. Our pricing aligns closely with DigitalOcean’s rates, averaging about $0.50 per CPU hour.

Compute costs are paid using your USDC Balance. Do not confuse this with your Token Balance. (see section below)
Looking ahead, Simmate plans to integrate HPC clusters from partner companies and universities. Once available, these will reduce compute costs drastically. There are also plans to allow users to provide their own resources for their own calculations or even others to utilize. For the latter, Simmate would compensate contributed resources by adding to their USDC balance. Reach out if you are interested in early testing of these features.

Digital Currencies

USDC

1 USDC = $1 for compute costs & workflows

USDC is used to pay for compute costs when you are using Simmate's computational resources, rather than supplying your own.

Configure your preferred payment method on your Profile page, where you are given the following options:

  • Stripe Checkout (card, Google Pay, Apple Pay, etc.)

    This is the most straightforward method as it supports familiar payment options. Simmate enforces a minimum payment of $25 for Stripe transactions to negate Stripe’s fees and to encourage users to switch to USDC.

  • USDC stablecoin

    Pegged 1:1 with the USD, USDC enables secure, peer-to-peer, and worldwide payments on the Ethereum blockchain. Simmate does not enforce any minimum payment with USDC. Currently, we only support USDC on Ethereum and Base chains, but reach out if you would like us to add support for other stablecoins and/or chains. And if you are new to cryptocurrencies and USDC, we recommend that beginners use the Base app to create a wallet and send USDC.

  • Venmo (not recommended)

    In the US, Venmo is well established and convienient method for small transactions. Just like with Stripe, Simmate enforces a minimum payment of $25 for Venmo transactions to negate Venmo’s fees and to encourage users to switch to USDC. Payment made via Venmo must be manually added to the Simmate system by a member of our team - so there will be a delay in the funds showing in your balance. For this reason, we ask that users fund via Venmo only as a last resort.

Tokens

1 Token = $1 of estimated costs outside of Simmate

Simmate tokens have no physical value and are only used for planning and budgeting. Your token balance is unlimited since Tokens are purely theoretical, and you can adjust your balance in the navbar.

As an example of how tokens are used, let's say you submit a molecule to the "Synthesis Tracker" app, indicating that you plan to run a synthesis of it. This costs nothing to submit & track in the website, but you may still want to record the total costs of actually running this synthesis in the lab (e.g., labor, reagents, etc.). You track these costs using Tokens. And as you record many syntheses and assay runs, you will be able to track real-world costs dedicated to specific projects, regions chemical chemical space, or specific hypotheses.
