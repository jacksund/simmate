Frequently Asked Questions

Privacy

You can install Simmate locally to run private workflows and even set up a private server. But as far as the public website goes: no, you can not make any of your results private. In the spirit of building a community that openly shares chemistry data, everything in our public website will remain openly accessible to everyone.

We aim to be as transparent as possible with our finances. This is because, as an open collective, we want users to know that the science & our community comes first - and that all funds going through Simmate are fairly distributed. A public ledger might be suprising, but it is by no means revolutionary. Simmate uses pseudonymity -- where all transactions are public, but user identities are not. This is exactly how many cryptocurrencies operate, including the most popular currencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum.

  • Email & Name: only your email and (if it's available) your name are stored. We will never send you promotional emails or newsletters.
  • Country/State: this is only required for purchases made on Simmate, and we only need this information for tax purposes. We are required to collect this by "Know your Customer" regulations, but if there's ever the option, we will gladly remove this from our data collection.
  • Website Traffic: every new page visit is logged so that we can go back and scale cloud resources appropriately, see what features users are most interested in, and utilize stats for grants/funding.
Finances

Our platform operates on a pay-as-you-go model. Costs are transparent and determined by the underlying decentralized compute resources consumed (CPU/GPU time, storage, network). The platform itself takes a minimal transaction fee (typically less than 5%). You can drastically reduce or eliminate these costs by running tasks on your own compute node.

To use your own hardware, simply download and deploy our **Compute Node Client**. Once registered, you can route your personal workflows directly to your node ID. This means you only incur your local operational costs (electricity, internet) and bypass the fees associated with renting compute from the decentralized network.

There are two primary ways to earn USDC:
  1. Compute Provision: Run a secure compute node and earn fees for every task executed on your machine.
  2. Workflow Creation: Develop and publish popular, high-value workflows. You earn a royalty every time another user executes your published workflow.
All earnings are automatically distributed via smart contracts directly to your linked wallet.

Simmate is completely free. If you decide to download and host your own server, your team may want to use a cloud provider like DigitalOcean. These extra cloud-hosting features are 100% optional because every component for Simmate is built on free and open-source software.
